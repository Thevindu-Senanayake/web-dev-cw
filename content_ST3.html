Field Guide · Chapter 3

Soil & Sustainable Land

Healthy soil is the foundation of life on land - and one of the easiest things for anyone to protect. Here's the threat, what recovery looks like, and your move.

  • Living Soil
  • Land Degradation
  • Regenerative Practices
  • How You Can Act
The stakes

Living Soil

A single handful of healthy soil contains billions of organisms that recycle nutrients and store water. This living layer feeds nearly all our crops and locks away carbon, making it one of the planet's most important - and overlooked - resources.

The threat

Land Degradation

Over-farming, chemical overuse, and erosion strip soil of life and nutrients. Degraded land grows less food, holds less water, and releases stored carbon. Roughly a third of the world's soil is already degraded.

The recovery

Regenerative Practices

Cover crops, crop rotation, composting, and reduced tillage rebuild soil health over time. These regenerative methods bring back organic matter, support pollinators, and make farmland more productive without chemicals.

Your move

How You Can Act

Compost your food scraps, buy from regenerative or local farms, avoid peat-based products, and grow something - even a windowsill herb. Every bit of living soil you nurture supports the wider land ecosystem.
