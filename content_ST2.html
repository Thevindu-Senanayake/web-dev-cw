Field Guide · Chapter 3

Wildlife & Biodiversity

The web of species that keeps land ecosystems alive - and why protecting it protects us. Here's the threat, what recovery looks like, and your move.

  • The Value of Biodiversity
  • Species Under Pressure
  • Creating Havens
  • How You Can Act
The stakes

The Value of Biodiversity

Biodiversity is the variety of life - from soil microbes to apex predators. Each species plays a role in pollination, pest control, and nutrient cycling. A rich mix of species makes ecosystems more resilient to drought, disease, and climate shocks.

The threat

Species Under Pressure

Habitat loss, pollution, and invasive species are pushing wildlife toward extinction faster than ever. When one species vanishes, others that depend on it suffer too. Pollinators like bees are an early warning sign of ecosystems in trouble.

The recovery

Creating Havens

Wildlife corridors, protected reserves, and even small garden habitats give species room to feed and breed. Connecting fragmented green spaces lets animals move safely and keeps gene pools healthy.

Your move

How You Can Act

Plant native flowers, leave wild corners in gardens, avoid pesticides, and support conservation groups. Recording wildlife sightings through citizen-science apps also helps scientists track and protect species.
