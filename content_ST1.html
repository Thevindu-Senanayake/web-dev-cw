Field Guide · Chapter 3

Forests & Reforestation

Why the world's forests are central to Life on Land - and how anyone can help bring them back. Here's the threat, what recovery looks like, and your move.

  • Why Forests Matter
  • Threats
  • Restoration
  • How You Can Act
The stakes

Why Forests Matter

Forests cover about a third of the planet's land and are home to most terrestrial species. They clean our air, regulate rainfall, and store vast amounts of carbon. When a forest thrives, the soil, water, and wildlife around it thrive too - which is exactly what SDG 15 aims to protect.

The threat

Threats to Forests

Deforestation for farming, logging, and development removes millions of hectares each year. Once cleared, land loses its ability to hold water and carbon, and the species that depended on it disappear. Fragmented forests are far more vulnerable to fire and disease.

The recovery

Restoration in Action

Reforestation and natural regeneration can bring degraded land back to life. Community-led planting projects, protected corridors, and rewilding all help forests recover. The key is planting the right native species and giving them time to mature.

Your move

How You Can Act

You don't need to own land to help. Support reforestation charities, choose recycled paper, cut back on products linked to deforestation, and join a local planting day. Small, repeated choices add up across a whole community.
